Catalog & structure I do it

Make the one store hold every layer of what you sell.

Create 5 collections in Products → Collections: White-Label Formulas, Raw Materials & Actives, Packaging & Components, Contract Manufacturing, Formulation Services.

Add packaging products — bottles, tops, jars, pumps, cartons; variants by size/finish.

Your 29 white-label SKUs are already there — just tag and slot them into the collection.
Your real per-unit wholesale prices, packaging SKUs, and raw-material costs are the one thing I need from you to fill this in. Needs you
2

The wholesale engine App

Self-serve login, your pricing, MOQs, reorder, quotes — without Shopify Plus.

Install a B2B app. Recommended: SparkLayer (full B2B storefront + quotes) or Wholesale Gorilla / BSS B2B for a lighter start. Needs you to approve the subscription.
Registration + approval — practices request access; you approve. Only approved accounts see wholesale pricing.
Price lists / customer groups — each practice sees their negotiated price ($14, etc.), hidden from the public.
MOQ & increments per product — tiered minimums (“min 250, by 250”), exactly like the prototype.
Reorder + Request-a-Quote — saved lists for fast reorders; quote requests for custom formulation / contract-mfg projects.
3

Payments — wire-first, no terms I do it

Built around how your buyers actually pay, and protecting your cash.

Wire / ACH — add a custom manual method in Settings → Payments → Manual methods with your bank details. Orders arrive Payment pending; you mark paid when funds land, then ship. Zero fees.
Affirm (pay over time) — turn on Shop Pay Installments in Settings → Payments. The buyer pays monthly; you're paid in full up front, Affirm carries it. Needs you to confirm Shopify Payments details.
Bigger invoices — optionally add a B2B financing provider (Resolve, Balance, or Wisetack) that pays you upfront and assumes the risk. App
Card — kept on for anyone who'll accept the ~2.9% fee. Not the default.
We never enable net terms from you. A bank extends the credit, not The Cosmetic Formulary — so you're always paid before product leaves.

The website I do it

Turn the prototype you've been clicking into the real, working storefront.

Your back office I do it

The login for your team — and it already exists.

Add your 3 employees as staff in Settings → Users and permissions, with role-based access. This is the back office: admin.shopify.com.
The daily loop: Orders → new wholesale order or quote → confirm wire received → print label → ship. Customers → approve practices, set their pricing.
Optional ops dashboard — I can build a one-screen “what's due / what to rebatch / run-rate” view on top of Shopify, tied into your Formulary OS. I do it
