# The TCF Standard — Reformulation Marks
### Single Source of Truth · v1.0 · locked 2026-06-08

This document is the **canonical definition** of the TCF Standard and its seven Reformulation Marks.
It is intellectual property and the strongest differentiator on the site. **Nothing about a Mark — its
name, label, glyph, definition, or proof line — may change anywhere (card, PDP, collection legend,
marketing, decks) without updating THIS document first and bumping the version.** If the site and this
doc ever disagree, this doc wins.

Live code source today: `snippets/tcf-standard.liquid` (renders the Marks) and the legend in
`sections/tcf-collection.liquid`. These must always match the strings below verbatim.

---

## The framework has two layers — keep them separate

- **Layer 1 — The Lens (Evaluation Standards):** the six measures we judge a formula against. This is
  *credibility* — the "by what standard." It belongs on the About / Standards page and the catalog
  explainer, **not** on every product.
- **Layer 2 — The Action (Reformulation Marks):** a **closed set of seven** things we may have done to a
  formula. This is what appears **on the product card and PDP**. Each product lights up only the **3–5
  that apply** — same vocabulary, every time. Think nutrition panel: learn the badges once, read every
  product in two seconds.

## Layer 1 · The six TCF Evaluation Standards (the Lens)

| # | Standard | Definition |
|---|----------|------------|
| 01 | **Sensory Experience** | How it feels in the hand and on the skin. |
| 02 | **Treatment Performance** | How effectively it delivers its intended result. |
| 03 | **Formula Elegance** | Texture, glide, absorption, finish, sophistication. |
| 04 | **Patient Experience** | Comfort, irritation profile, daily usability. |
| 05 | **Ingredient Strategy** | Modern ingredients chosen for performance and skin compatibility. |
| 06 | **Repurchase Potential** | The likelihood users keep coming back to it. |

## Layer 2 · The seven Reformulation Marks (the closed vocabulary)

Every product is tagged from **only** these seven. Each Mark has: a **glyph** (stable identifier), a
**display label** (scannable, on cards), a **short label** (for tight UI), the **Standard it maps to**
(credibility), the **exact definition**, and the **proof line** (substantiation, shown on expand).

| Glyph | Display label | Short label | Maps to (Layer 1) |
|:---:|---|---|---|
| ① | Modern Actives | Actives | Ingredient Strategy |
| ② | Elevated Sensory | Sensory | Sensory Experience · Formula Elegance |
| ③ | Low-Irritant Base | Low-Irritant | Patient Experience |
| ④ | Optimized Delivery | Delivery | Treatment Performance |
| ⑤ | Barrier Supported | Barrier | Patient Experience |
| ⑥ | Stability Verified | Stability | Treatment Performance |
| ⑦ | Daily-Use Tuned | Daily-Use | Repurchase Potential |

### Per-Mark detail (definition + exact proof line)

**① Modern Actives** — *Ingredient Strategy*
- Definition: legacy actives swapped for current-generation, better-tolerated equivalents at efficacious levels.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Swapped legacy actives for current-generation, better-tolerated equivalents at efficacious levels."

**② Elevated Sensory** — *Sensory Experience · Formula Elegance*
- Definition: texture, glide, slip and finish reworked for a lighter, more refined feel.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Reworked texture, glide, slip and finish for a lighter, more refined feel."

**③ Low-Irritant Base** — *Patient Experience*
- Definition: common sensitizers removed or limited.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Removed or limited common sensitizers — no added fragrance, dyes, or drying alcohol."

**④ Optimized Delivery** — *Treatment Performance*
- Definition: actives penetrate and stay stable so the dose performs on skin.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Improved how the actives penetrate and stay stable, so the dose actually performs on skin."

**⑤ Barrier Supported** — *Patient Experience*
- Definition: barrier lipids/humectants added and pH buffered to lower sting and tightness.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Added barrier lipids and humectants and buffered the pH to lower sting and post-use tightness."

**⑥ Stability Verified** — *Treatment Performance*
- Definition: preservation and packaging updated so potency holds.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Updated preservation and packaging so potency holds from first use to last."

**⑦ Daily-Use Tuned** — *Repurchase Potential*
- Definition: everyday feel dialed in so patients keep using — and repurchasing.
- **Proof line (verbatim):** "Dialed in the everyday feel so patients keep using it — and keep repurchasing."

## The standard card / PDP anatomy (same on every product)

1. **Product type** — category label (sets the benefit vocabulary).
2. **Product title** — the TCF product name.
3. **Benchmark Product** — the named category anchor it stands in for (e.g., *Obagi Medical*). A reference
   point that tells the buyer the tier — **not** a head-to-head claim.
4. **Signature line** — always identical: **"◆ Reengineered to TCF Standards."**
5. **Marks** — 3–5 chips from the fixed seven. The two-second read.
6. **Why we reformulated** — expands to the **proof lines** for that product's Marks (substantiation).
   Collapsed on the card; **open by default on the PDP.**
7. **Plain-terms benefit** — one human sentence, tuned by category (below).

---

## The Benchmark rule (the line that keeps this defensible)

> **Never say "better than [brand]." Say what we did to our formula.**

- The **Benchmark Product** names a category anchor so a buyer knows the tier — it is *not* a superiority
  claim (which would require comparative testing).
- **Every Mark describes our own formula** ("added barrier lipids," "no added fragrance") — all verifiable
  from TCF batch records.
- "Modernized / reengineered" lands without denigrating the other product. Clean with lawyers and dermatologists.

## Mark assignment logic

- A product shows **only the Marks that are true for it** (3–5).
- **Per-product override:** product metafield **`custom.tcf_marks`** = comma-separated mark numbers
  (e.g. `2,3,5,7`). When present, it wins.
- **Default (when no metafield):** by product category —

| Category | Marks |
|---|---|
| Serum | ① ② ④ ⑥ ⑦ |
| Oil | ② ⑤ ⑦ |
| Cleanser | ② ③ ⑤ ⑦ |
| Cleansing Oil | ② ③ ⑦ |
| Exfoliator | ① ② ③ ⑦ |
| Cream / Lotion / Moisturizer / Aqueous Gel | ② ④ ⑤ ⑦ |
| SPF / Sunscreen | ② ③ ④ ⑦ |
| Toner / Toner Pad | ① ③ ⑦ |
| Mask | ② ⑤ ⑦ |
| Eye Cream | ② ④ ⑤ ⑦ |
| Body Oil / Body Scrub | ② ③ ⑦ |
| Lip Balm | ② ⑤ ⑦ |
| (fallback) | ② ③ ⑤ ⑦ |

> Defaults are a defensible starting point. To make a SKU exact, set `custom.tcf_marks` on that product.

## Icon strategy

- **Canonical icon = circled numerals ①②③④⑤⑥⑦** (Unicode U+2460–U+2466), rendered in **navy
  (`--tcf-navy #1E2936`)** alongside the label.
- Rationale: nutrition-panel learnability, perfect consistency, zero asset dependency, scales everywhere.
- **The number is the stable identifier.** Bespoke line-icons MAY be introduced later, but each must keep
  its **exact number and display label** — the number never gets reused or reordered.

---

## Allowed usage

- Marks appear on: **product cards, the PDP, the collection-page legend**, and may appear in decks/marketing.
- A Mark may be shown **only when it is true** for that formula and **verifiable from batch records**.
- Marks **always describe the TCF formula** — never the benchmark, never as a comparison.
- The **Signature line is fixed**: "Reengineered to TCF Standards." Do not paraphrase it per product.
- **Closed set:** never invent an eighth Mark or a one-off badge. If a new idea recurs, it must be added
  here as a versioned change first.

## Banned language (anywhere Marks/benchmarks appear)

`dupe` · `clone` · `copy` · `exact match` · `same as [brand]` · `better than [brand]` · `superior to [brand]`
· "done right" / "what it should have been" (retired) · unsupported clinical/medical-outcome claims ·
factory/manufacturing language as the lead (allowed only as a small note, and only on the Contract
Manufacturing page).
